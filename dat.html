DAT Solutions — OnTime

A real-time freight tracking platform

Modernizing a 40+ year old company

DAT Solutions operates the United States' largest freight marketplace — also known as "load boards". Load boards help truck drivers, brokers, and shippers keep our roads loaded with freight. Think of a load board as a "Craigslist for truckers", a place where brokers and shippers list their freight and truckers (typically owner-operators) post their availability.

I was one of three visual designers assigned the task of giving DAT's entire product suite a visual and UX overhaul using Google's Material Design as a starting point. It felt like a herculean task as some products hadn't been updated in over a decade, but the first product I took on was their newest, OnTime.

OnTime

What is OnTime?

OnTime is a product that allows freight brokers and shippers to track drivers carrying their freight. Drivers download OnTime's app on their phones and are tracked for the duration of a shipment via GPS. Brokers and shippers check OnTime's web app to see real time updates regarding a freight's location. OnTime sets itself apart from competitors with features like in-app messaging, real time location updates, and giving truckers control of their privacy when they are off duty.

Defining Goals

I worked closely alongside OnTime's product team to refine their vision and goals. This included created mockups and prototypes for key features such as in-app messaging, which we hoped would reduce check calls and turning GPS tracking off and on, so drivers could take control of their privacy.

Reflection

UX portfolios are typically places where you will find seamless storytelling, with cool photos of sticky notes from affinity mapping, clear problems, and clear solutions. OnTime was not one of those projects.

Hot Market Maps

What is it?

Hot Market Maps is a tool that allows users to see the load-to-truck ration of within the continental US and Canada. I was one of the three designs who gave Hot Market Maps its first design refresh in 9 years. Version 2 of Hot Market Maps allows for further granularity and insights of load-to-truck ratios.
